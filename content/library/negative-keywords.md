## Start with intent

Ask whether the shopper’s intent can reasonably be satisfied by the advertised product. Clear mismatches—wrong product class, incompatible size, or unrelated use case—can be blocked quickly.

## Choose the right negative type

### Negative exact

Use negative exact to block one specific query while preserving related discovery. It is usually the safer choice for routing a proven query from research into exact targeting.

### Negative phrase

Use negative phrase only when the phrase itself represents a consistently invalid intent. Its broader reach creates a greater risk of blocking valuable variations.

## Evidence thresholds

| Situation | Evidence needed | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Clearly irrelevant | One verified query | Negate promptly |
| Relevant, no orders | Spend relative to expected CPA | Bid down, wait, or negate |
| Converting but inefficient | Multiple orders preferred | Lower bid before blocking |
| Harvested to exact | Stable exact target and routing need | Negative exact in source |

## Conflict check

Before applying a negative, search the account for existing targets and negatives. Confirm campaign scope, match type, and whether the change could block a strategic exact target.

## Decision record

For every batch, capture query, campaign, negative type, evidence window, spend, orders, rationale, and reviewer. This makes reversals possible when listing economics or assortment changes.

## Review checklist

- [ ] Relevance was assessed before performance.
- [ ] Attribution delay is complete.
- [ ] The negative match type is no broader than necessary.
- [ ] Routing destination exists and is active.
- [ ] No high-priority query will be blocked.
