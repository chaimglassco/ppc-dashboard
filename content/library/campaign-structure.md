## Purpose and outcome

Use this SOP when launching or rebuilding Sponsored Products campaigns. The outcome is a structure that makes query control, budgeting, reporting, and handoff straightforward.

## Inputs required

- Advertised ASINs and variation strategy
- Margin-informed target ACOS
- Priority keywords and product targets
- Daily budget envelope
- Brand and category naming rules

## Naming convention

Use `SP | Market | Product | Targeting | Match | Intent`. For example: `SP | US | Trail Bottle | Keyword | Exact | Scale`.

Keep names readable. Put volatile settings such as bids and dates in the campaign notes or change log, not the name.

## Build sequence

### Discovery layer

Create one automatic campaign and one broad or phrase research campaign per tightly related product group. Apply negatives that prevent known branded or irrelevant leakage.

### Performance layer

Place proven queries in exact-match campaigns. Separate branded, category, competitor, and defensive intent when their economics or budgets differ.

### Product targeting layer

Separate category expansion, competitor ASINs, complements, and own-ASIN defense. This preserves clear bid and placement decisions.

## Budget guardrails

| Layer | Starting allocation | Primary job |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Exact performance | 45–60% | Capture proven demand |
| Discovery | 15–25% | Find new converting terms |
| Product targeting | 15–25% | Expand and defend |
| Experiments | 5–10% | Test bounded hypotheses |

## Quality-control checklist

- [ ] Every campaign has a single explainable job.
- [ ] Products in one ad group share search intent.
- [ ] Match types are visible in naming.
- [ ] Exact campaigns receive sufficient budget.
- [ ] Negative routing does not block the intended target.
- [ ] Portfolio and campaign names follow the standard.

## Handoff record

Record launch date, owner, target ACOS, budget rationale, source keyword set, negatives, and the first review date. Schedule the first search-term review after enough traffic accumulates, not at an arbitrary click count.
