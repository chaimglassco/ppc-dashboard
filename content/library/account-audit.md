## Define scope and baseline

- [ ] Confirm marketplaces, portfolios, products, and date range.
- [ ] Record sales, spend, ACOS, TACOS, orders, CPC, and conversion rate.
- [ ] Separate branded and non-branded performance.
- [ ] Note seasonality, promotions, and inventory constraints.

## Retail readiness

- [ ] Identify stockouts, suppressed listings, and Buy Box loss.
- [ ] Review titles, main images, reviews, ratings, and price.
- [ ] Confirm priority products index for target queries.
- [ ] Flag variation or catalog issues that fragment performance.

## Structure and targeting

- [ ] Every campaign has a clear purpose.
- [ ] Match types and intents are distinguishable.
- [ ] Search terms are being harvested and routed.
- [ ] Product targeting includes defense and expansion where justified.
- [ ] Duplicate targets do not create unmanageable overlap.

## Bids, placements, and budgets

| Area | Audit question | Risk signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bids | Do bids reflect conversion economics? | Uniform or stale bids |
| Placements | Are multipliers evidence-based? | High top-of-search spend without return |
| Budgets | Does spend reach proven demand? | Efficient campaigns capped early |
| Pacing | Is monthly spend controlled? | Late-month emergency cuts |

## Measurement and operations

- [ ] Targets and KPIs are documented.
- [ ] Change history includes reasons and owners.
- [ ] Reporting separates paid and total business outcomes.
- [ ] Experiments have hypotheses and review dates.

## Prioritize findings

Score each finding by expected impact, confidence, effort, and urgency. Deliver the top five actions with owners and dates rather than an unranked inventory of issues.
