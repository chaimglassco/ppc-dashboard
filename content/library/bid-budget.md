## Establish economic guardrails

Define target ACOS from contribution margin and business objective. A launch campaign may temporarily accept a higher target, but the exception needs a ceiling and review date.

## Estimate a viable bid

A useful starting relationship is `target CPC = expected conversion rate × target cost per order`. Treat this as a guardrail, not a guarantee: auctions, placements, and conversion rates change.

## Bid adjustment procedure

1. Use a window that contains enough clicks and completed attribution.
2. Separate placement effects where they are material.
3. Compare actual CPC and conversion rate with the economic guardrail.
4. Make a bounded change proportional to confidence.
5. Record the new bid and the next review date.

## Budget allocation order

### Fund proven demand

Protect campaigns that are profitable, conversion-stable, and regularly budget constrained.

### Preserve discovery

Maintain a controlled research allowance. Discovery should not consume money needed by proven exact targets, but eliminating it can starve future growth.

### Bound experiments

Give each experiment a fixed budget, hypothesis, owner, and stopping rule.

## Pacing table

| Pace vs plan | Interpretation | Response |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| Below 85% | Underpacing | Check traffic, bids, eligibility, and caps |
| 85–105% | On plan | Monitor; avoid needless edits |
| Above 105% | Overpacing | Cut lowest marginal value first |

## Safety checks

- [ ] Do not raise budgets on inefficient campaigns merely to avoid caps.
- [ ] Do not combine a large bid and placement increase in one step.
- [ ] Recheck automation rules before manual changes.
- [ ] Protect branded and defensive coverage deliberately, not automatically.
