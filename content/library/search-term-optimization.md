## Set the review window

Choose a window long enough to include the buying cycle and meaningful spend. Seven days may work for a high-volume product; low-volume products often need 30–60 days. Exclude periods with stockouts or abnormal promotions when they distort the decision.

## Prepare the report

1. Export the search term report at the required account scope.
2. Normalize query casing and whitespace.
3. Join product margin or target ACOS where available.
4. Calculate conversion rate, CPC, and spend per order.
5. Label branded, category, competitor, and irrelevant intent.

## Classify each search term

### Scale

A relevant term with repeated profitable orders may deserve a higher bid, placement adjustment, dedicated exact target, or additional budget.

### Maintain

Stable terms near the goal need monitoring, not constant intervention. Small bid edits can create more noise than value.

### Reduce

Relevant but inefficient terms can remain useful at a lower CPC. Estimate a bid from target conversion economics rather than applying the same percentage cut everywhere.

### Negate

Negate clearly irrelevant traffic immediately. For relevant traffic, account for expected value and sample size before blocking future exposure.

## Decision table

| Evidence | Likely action | Validation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Profitable repeat orders | Harvest or scale | Check query duplication |
| Relevant, high spend, no orders | Reduce or negate | Review CVR and listing fit |
| One expensive click | Usually wait | Check CPC anomaly |
| Irrelevant intent | Negative exact/phrase | Confirm scope |
| Strong orders, budget capped | Reallocate budget | Confirm marginal efficiency |

## Close the loop

Log the old value, new value, reason, owner, and review date. Revisit meaningful changes after the chosen attribution window. The goal is a decision trail, not a large number of daily edits.
