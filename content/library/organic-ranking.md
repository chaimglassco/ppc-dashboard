## The ranking system in plain language

Amazon’s search system tries to show products that are relevant to a query and likely to satisfy the shopper. Organic position is therefore an output of multiple signals, not a score that advertisers can edit directly.

> PPC can accelerate qualified traffic and sales, but it cannot rescue an irrelevant or poorly converting detail page indefinitely.

## The three signal groups

### Relevance and indexing

Amazon must first associate the product with the query. Build relevance through accurate titles, bullets, descriptions, backend terms, category attributes, and shopper language. Confirm indexing before diagnosing rank movement.

### Conversion performance

Competitive price, Prime availability, reviews, imagery, variation structure, and offer health determine how efficiently visits become orders. Compare unit session percentage to close substitutes rather than to an account-wide average.

### Sales history and velocity

Recent sales demonstrate demand. Consistent qualified sales are generally more durable than a short, inefficient spike. Seasonality, stockouts, price changes, and competitor promotions can change the required velocity.

## How PPC supports organic growth

1. Target queries that accurately describe the product.
2. Use exact-match campaigns to control high-priority terms.
3. Review search terms for conversion evidence, not clicks alone.
4. Move proven language into the listing where appropriate.
5. Track organic position separately from paid placement.

## Diagnostic sequence

| Check | Evidence | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Indexed? | Query test and listing fields | Repair relevance gaps |
| Retail ready? | Buy Box, inventory, price, reviews | Resolve offer friction |
| Converting? | Sessions, orders, unit session % | Improve detail page |
| Enough demand? | Search volume and category context | Reset the target |
| PPC efficient? | Search-term CVR and CPC | Refine targeting and bids |

## Weekly review checklist

- [ ] Confirm priority products remain in stock and indexed.
- [ ] Record organic rank for the same query set and location.
- [ ] Separate branded from non-branded movement.
- [ ] Annotate price, coupon, content, and review changes.
- [ ] Compare paid sales growth with total sales growth.

## Common interpretation errors

Do not treat correlation as proof. A rank increase after a bid change may also reflect seasonality, a competitor stockout, or a promotion. Use consistent observations and change one major variable at a time when possible.
